Customer Support Representative

Salary: $30,000 - $45,000/year -- This is the most accessible remote job in 2026. Companies of all sizes need people to answer customer questions via email, chat, and phone. No degree required. Training is provided by the employer.

What You Will Do

  • Answer customer questions via email, live chat, or phone
  • Troubleshoot basic product or service issues
  • Process orders, returns, and refunds
  • Escalate complex issues to specialized teams
  • Document interactions in a CRM system

Skills You Need

  • Clear written and verbal communication
  • Patience and empathy
  • Basic computer skills (email, web browsers, typing 40+ WPM)
  • Ability to follow scripts and procedures

Where to Apply

Amazon, Shopify, Zendesk, HubSpot, and hundreds of startups hire remote support reps. Check FlexJobs, We Work Remotely, and LinkedIn daily for new listings.

Data Entry Specialist

Salary: $28,000 - $40,000/year -- Data entry is straightforward: you enter information into databases, spreadsheets, or software systems. It requires accuracy and speed, not experience. Many positions are part-time or flexible.

What You Will Do

  • Input data from paper or digital documents into systems
  • Verify and clean existing data for accuracy
  • Maintain databases and spreadsheets
  • Generate reports from entered data

Skills You Need

  • Fast and accurate typing (50+ WPM preferred)
  • Basic spreadsheet knowledge (Google Sheets or Excel)
  • Attention to detail
  • Ability to follow instructions precisely

Virtual Assistant

Salary: $25,000 - $50,000/year -- Virtual assistants handle administrative tasks for business owners, executives, or entrepreneurs. The pay range is wide because VAs who specialize (real estate, e-commerce, marketing) command significantly higher rates.

What You Will Do

  • Manage email inboxes and calendars
  • Schedule meetings and coordinate travel
  • Handle basic bookkeeping and invoicing
  • Research tasks and data organization
  • Social media posting and community management

Skills You Need

  • Strong organizational skills
  • Comfort with tools like Google Workspace, Slack, Zoom, and Notion
  • Proactive communication
  • Ability to prioritize and manage multiple tasks

Where to Apply

Belay, Time Etc, Zirtual, and Fancy Hands hire remote VAs. You can also find VA positions on Upwork and Fiverr as a freelance pathway.

Social Media Coordinator

Salary: $32,000 - $48,000/year -- If you already spend time on social media, you have more transferable skills than you think. Companies need people to create content, schedule posts, respond to comments, and grow their audience. A personal social media presence can serve as your portfolio.

What You Will Do

  • Create and schedule social media posts across platforms
  • Respond to comments and messages
  • Track engagement metrics and report on performance
  • Research trends and hashtags
  • Collaborate with marketing teams on campaigns

Skills You Need

  • Understanding of major social platforms (Instagram, TikTok, X, LinkedIn)
  • Basic graphic design (Canva is sufficient)
  • Strong writing and communication
  • Familiarity with scheduling tools (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later)

Transcriptionist

Salary: $25,000 - $45,000/year -- Transcription involves converting audio or video recordings into written text. General transcription requires no special training. Medical and legal transcription pay more but may require certification.

What You Will Do

  • Listen to audio/video recordings and type what you hear
  • Follow formatting guidelines provided by the client
  • Proofread and edit transcripts for accuracy
  • Meet deadlines for turnaround times

Skills You Need

  • Excellent listening skills and fast typing (60+ WPM ideal)
  • Strong grammar and punctuation
  • Ability to work with transcription software
  • Quiet work environment

Where to Apply

Rev, TranscribeMe, GoTranscript, and Scribie all hire beginners. Rev pays $0.30-$1.10 per audio minute and has no experience requirements.

Online Tutor

Pay: $20 - $40/hour -- If you are knowledgeable in any school subject, test prep area, or language, you can tutor online. No teaching degree required for most platforms. This is one of the highest-paying entry-level remote options because it is based on knowledge, not credentials.

What You Will Do

  • Teach students one-on-one via video call
  • Create lesson plans and practice exercises
  • Track student progress and adjust approach
  • Provide homework help and test preparation

Skills You Need

  • Strong knowledge of your subject area
  • Clear communication and patience
  • Reliable internet and webcam
  • Ability to explain concepts at different levels

Where to Apply

Tutor.com, Wyzant, Chegg, Preply, and iTalki (for languages) all hire tutors with no formal teaching experience. Set competitive rates and build reviews to grow your client base.

How to Get Hired With No Experience

Getting your first remote job is the hardest. After that, each application gets easier. Here is how to break in:

1

Build a Clean, ATS-Friendly Resume

Use the free Resume Builder to create a resume that passes automated screening systems. Focus on transferable skills, not job titles. Organized a church event? That is project management. Helped friends with tech problems? That is customer support experience.

2

Highlight Transferable Skills

Every life experience teaches skills that employers value. Communication, organization, problem-solving, time management, and attention to detail apply to almost every remote job. Reframe your experience around these core competencies.

3

Complete Free Certifications

Google offers free certifications in IT Support, Data Analytics, Project Management, and UX Design through Coursera. HubSpot offers free certifications in marketing and sales. These take 2-8 weeks and immediately boost your resume.

4

Apply in Volume With Customization

Apply to 5-10 positions per day. Customize the first two lines of your cover letter for each company. Mention the company name, what they do, and why you want to work there specifically. Generic applications get ignored.

5

Set Up a Professional Online Presence

Create a LinkedIn profile with a professional photo and headline like "Customer Support Specialist | Remote Work Enthusiast." Join remote work communities and engage with posts from companies you want to work for.
